About the Five Dimensions

The Economist Intelligence Unit's Democracy Index measures political systems worldwide across five dimensions:

  • Electoral Process & Pluralism — free and fair elections, universal suffrage
  • Functioning of Government — checks and balances, transparency
  • Political Participation — voter turnout, citizen engagement
  • Political Culture — democratic values, peaceful transitions
  • Civil Liberties — free speech, association, rule of law

For this analysis, all regime labels were removed, giving ML algorithms access to raw scores only — a truly unsupervised scenario.

Key Research Insights

Natural Patterns Emerge

ML algorithms independently discovered four distinct clusters aligning closely with expert-defined regime types — validating that democratic classifications reflect real, inherent structure in political data.

Critical Dimensions

Civil liberties (0.90 correlation) and electoral processes (0.86) emerged as the most powerful predictors of regime type, showing which aspects most strongly differentiate political systems.

Early Warning Signals

Countries like Hungary, Poland, and Mexico occupy a statistical gray zone between flawed democracies and hybrid regimes — suggesting ML could serve as an early-warning system for democratic backsliding.
